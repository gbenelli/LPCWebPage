Description about the scientists

Christos Leonidopoulos
Christos Leonidopoulos graduated with a PhD from Princeton and spent four years as a postdoc at Columbia before becoming a research physics staff at CERN. He was recently announced as a Senior LPC Fellow. He has served as deputy trigger coordinator at CMS since 2007 and has actively worked on and led the analysis effort to search for W' bosons at CMS since 2008. His recent search for the W' using the full 2010 dataset excluded masses below 1.58 TeV, setting the most stringent limits in the world.

Andrey Korytov
Andrey Korytov is Colonel Allan R. and Margaret G. Crow Professor at University of Florida at Gainesville. Andrey led the CMS Endcap Muon Chambers project from the earlier R&D stages starting in 1994 through the end of the construction in 2005. He was the co-convener of the CMS Higgs Physics Group; coordinating all Higgs boson searches in the CMS Collaboration for 2009-2010. He coordinates the UF group effort to prepare for discovering the Higgs boson via its 4-muon decay channel at LHC. He also leads the program of jet fragmentation studies at CDF.
